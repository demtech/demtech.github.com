For governments

Since governments and officials facilitates the entire democratic process, the importance of living up to demands from both legislation and universal democratic traditions, are critical for the public stakeholders. But besides formal requirements, a widespread trust in the democratic processes is paramount to ensure successful and valid elections. The research question of DemTech is concerned with this very matter; how to secure the social mechanisms of trust while creating processes with IT that can live up to the–almost unprecedented–high expectations.

How to collaborate

The research of DemTech is publicly available, meaning that any governmental institution, official or even citizen, are welcome to benefit from it. Apart from the published research DemTech have been involved with several governmental initiatives on digitalising different democratic processes (see the link to the Danish project or the cases below).

DemTech is an independent and impartial research group that focuses on bringing trustworthy technology to life. Because of the experiences in DemTech the governmental institutions benefits from neutral advise and proven technologies, while DemTech continues to explore the research question and draw on the experiences from the ministry. In the case of the Danish project for instance, DemTech and the Danish Ministry of the Interior signed a contract defining a framework for future cooperation.
