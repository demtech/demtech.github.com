For academia

The DemTech project is anchored in academia to provide unbiased and constructive input to both the research community and other interested. Since the area of democratic technology spans over so many academic traditions, DemTech have employed researchers within many different fields, ranging from social to natural sciences.


How to collaborate

DemTech contributes regularly to the academic society by publishing articles and participating in conferences around the globe. Since DemTech from time to time is participating in projects with governments or other organisations we have several researchers working on specific items which could be interesting for other researchers or research groups. These items can be found at the research page.

As a research group DemTech is lead by Carsten Schürmann. The research is mainly focused on two areas: Voting technology and Socio-technological studies.

Getting in touch

If you have questions or are interested in knowing more about our research and research areas, please write to Carsten at carsten@temtech.dk. If you wish to contact one or more researchers within a specific area, please refer to the list of researchers and research areas at the research page.
