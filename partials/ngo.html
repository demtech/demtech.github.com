For NGOs

From independent research institutes to interest groups, non-governmental organisations play an important role in developing and maintaining democratic institutions and practices. It is these roles and processes that DemTech is researching and wishes to elaborate. Because of the large amount of experience and knowledge within DemTech, any collaboration would be mutually beneficial.


How to collaborate

The research of DemTech is publicly available, meaning that any organisations are welcome to benefit from it. Like DemTech, some NGOs works on the grounds of neutrality to bring unbiased contemporary research into the public processes, which could be a basis for interesting common footing with the DemTech research group.

DemTech is in continuous contact with different organisations around the globe (see below) to stay up to date with current affairs and research. This knowledge is an important part of the work within DemTech, but also a resource to tap into for interested parties.

DemTech is an impartial research group that cannot participate in projects of economic character. However, it is still very much in the interest of DemTech to exchange information and possibly even collaborate to research new technology.
