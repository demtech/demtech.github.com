For industries

Governments, NGOs, academia and industry are very different players in a field where the new innovations of electronic democratic processes are evolving. But one goal is common for the four stakeholders: to create trustworthy and secure IT. Due to the extensive experience of DemTech and the span of the expertise within the research group–from cryptography and security to social sciences and the trust of voters–DemTech has a profound understanding of the many and heterogeneous challenges.


How to collaborate

The research of DemTech is publicly available, meaning that the industry in general are welcome to benefit from it. DemTech is also very interested in any new experiences and initiatives made from producers of technology associated with elections and democratic processes, and would be happy to communicate with such producers to exchange ideas and experiences for mutual benefit.

However, the research within DemTech is independent of any industrial interests and to protect this integrity DemTech cannot participate in any financial projects. Partnerships are thus not an option for us and we do not sell exclusive access to information. The researchers in DemTech strive to be as impartial as possible and to reply any inquiries from competing producers with detailed but consistent answers.
